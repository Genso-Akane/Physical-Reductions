# Heisenberg’s Uncertainty Principle
> **Status:** Operational Engineering Protocol

---

## 🔗 Causal Chain (Minimal)
`2. Event` → `20. Information` → `21. Measurement` → `8. System State`

**Logic:** Cause → Mechanism → Effect → Practical Conclusion

---

## ⚡ Cause
**21. Measurement**

---

## ⚙️ Mechanism
* **`20. Information` + `7. System` → `21. Measurement`**
* **`21. Measurement` → change of `8. System State`**
* **`2. Event` → `20. Information`**

Measurement is an event in which information is realized.
Realization of information requires participation of a system and changes the system state.

---

## 📡 Effect
* **`8. System State`** is not preserved under sequential measurements.
* **`20. Information`** about different parameters is realized through different events.
* Simultaneous fixation of complete information is impossible, since each measurement changes the system state.



---

## 🛠 Practical Conclusion
Limitation on joint precision is a consequence of **`21. Measurement`** → change of **`8. System State`**.
This is not a property of **`8. System State`**, but a regime of sequential **`21. Measurement`**.

**Engineering:**
* control is achieved through selection of the sequence of **`21. Measurement`**
* precision of one parameter increases at the expense of degradation of another
* optimization is tuning of order and tempo of **`9. Process`** of measurements

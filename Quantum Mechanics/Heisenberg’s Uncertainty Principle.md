# Heisenberg’s Uncertainty Principle
> **Status:** Operational Engineering Protocol  
> **Core Principle:** Limitation on joint precision as a consequence of `21. Measurement` modifying `8. System State`.

---

## 🔗 Causal Chain (Minimal)
`2. Event` → `20. Information` → `21. Measurement` → `8. System State`

**Logic:** Cause → Mechanism → Effect → Practical Conclusion

---

## ⚡ Cause
* **`21. Measurement`** — The act of state fixation.

---

## ⚙️ Mechanism
1. **`20. Information` + `7. System` → `21. Measurement`**: Measurement is an event in which information is realized through the interaction of the system and the observer.
2. **`21. Measurement` → Change of `8. System State`**: Every measurement is a physical event that inevitably modifies the state of the system.
3. **`2. Event` → `20. Information`**: Information is not a pre-existing entity; it is realized only as the structure of a specific event (measurement).

*Realization of information requires participation of a system and changes the system state.*

---

## 📡 Effect
* **`8. System State`** is not preserved under sequential measurements.
* **`20. Information`** about different parameters is realized through different, mutually exclusive events.
* Simultaneous fixation of complete information is impossible, since each measurement changes the system state before the next one can occur.

*Uncertainty is not a property of the System State, but a regime of sequential Measurement.*

---

## 🛠 Practical Conclusion (Engineering)
The limitation on joint precision is a physical cost of information realization.

* **Control:** Achieved through the strategic selection of the sequence of `21. Measurement`.
* **Precision:** Increasing the precision of one parameter happens at the expense of the degradation of another (state modification).
* **Optimization:** Tuning the order and `9. Process Tempo` of measurements to minimize unwanted state shifts.
